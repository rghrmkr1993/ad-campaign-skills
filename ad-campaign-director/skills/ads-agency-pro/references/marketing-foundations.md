# Marketing Foundations — Disciplines, Journeys, Objectives

The strategy-selection layer: which discipline, which journey model, which objective. Wrong
objective = the platform's AI optimizes for the wrong thing; everything downstream fails.

## Discipline map (28 disciplines → when they lead)

| Cluster | Disciplines | Leads when |
|---|---|---|
| Strategy core | Marketing strategy, digital, performance, brand, product, growth, revenue marketing | Always on — performance vs brand split is the first budget decision |
| Demand engine | Demand gen, customer acquisition, inbound, outbound, content, video | New-customer growth targets; long-cycle B2B leans inbound+demand gen |
| Relationship engine | Retention, lifecycle, email, SMS, WhatsApp, community, referral | LTV problems, repeat-purchase categories; cheaper than acquisition — check this FIRST when a client asks for "more sales" |
| Leverage plays | Affiliate, influencer, event, guerrilla, co-marketing | Multipliers on a working core funnel — never the core itself |
| Place-based | Local, hyperlocal, retail, omnichannel | Physical footprint, store visits, marketplace+D2C blends |

Rule: paid media is ONE lever. Diagnose before media-planning — if retention/lifecycle is
broken, more acquisition spend pours water into a leaking bucket. Say so to the client.

## Customer journey — canonical stages

Awareness → Interest → Consideration → Intent → Conversion → Retention → Upsell →
Cross-sell → Advocacy.

Map every campaign to stages it serves. A media plan that only buys Conversion-stage
audiences exhausts them; one that only buys Awareness never proves revenue.

## Journey models — pick per engagement

| Model | Structure | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| AIDA | Attention→Interest→Desire→Action | Single-ad and copy structure; creative reviews |
| TOFU/MOFU/BOFU | Funnel thirds | Budget splitting + content mapping (classic ecom/B2B) |
| Flywheel | Attract→Engage→Delight (momentum) | Retention-led brands, community brands, PLG |
| AARRR (Pirate) | Acquisition→Activation→Retention→Referral→Revenue | Apps, SaaS, startup growth reviews |
| See-Think-Do-Care | Intent-based audiences | Google-centric planning; maps cleanly to campaign types |
| RACE | Reach→Act→Convert→Engage | Client-facing plan documents; simple reporting narrative |

Default: TOFU/MOFU/BOFU for ecom, AARRR for apps/SaaS, See-Think-Do-Care for Google-heavy
mixes. Use ONE model per client plan; don't mix vocabularies in reports.

## Objective selection matrix

The objective you pick IS the instruction to the platform's AI. Choose by business goal,
never by vanity preference:

| Business goal | Platform objective | Optimization event | Trap to avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ecom revenue | Sales / Purchase | Purchase (not ATC) | Optimizing to ATC/checkout inflates numbers, kills ROAS |
| Lead pipeline | Leads | Qualified stage via offline import | Raw form-fills → junk leads; feed CRM quality back |
| App growth | App install/engagement | Activation or purchase event | Install-optimized = bot-adjacent junk |
| Footfall/local | Store visits / local campaigns / calls | Calls, direction requests | Needs GBP hygiene first |
| Launch/brand | Awareness/Reach/Video views | ThruPlay, reach | Judge on lift/branded search, not clicks |
| Retention/upsell | Remarketing + lifecycle channels | Repeat purchase, LTV | Don't pay to reach people email reaches free |
| Subscriptions | Sales + trial event | Trial→paid (optimize down-funnel) | Trial-optimized attracts serial trialers |

Two-question test before any campaign: (1) What single event, if it doubled, doubles the
client's business? (2) Can we measure it cleanly? That event is the optimization target.

## Channel-role assignment (per plan)

For each channel in the mix, write one line: `<channel> exists in this plan to <job> at
<stage>, judged by <metric>`. If you can't write that line, cut the channel.
