# Vertical Playbooks — Any Industry

This file has three parts:
1. **The Vertical Framework** — a repeatable method to build a playbook for ANY industry.
2. **Industry quick guides** — starting points for 12 common verticals.
3. **Worked examples** — four fully-built vertical playbooks showing the target depth.

---

# Part 1 — The Vertical Framework (use for any industry)

Answer these six questions for a new vertical. The answers ARE the playbook.

## 1. Regulatory & claims status
- Is this a restricted/special category on any platform (health, finance, housing,
  employment, politics, alcohol, gambling)? Special categories force limited targeting and
  extra review.
- What claims need substantiation? What words trigger rejection? (e.g., "treats/cures" in
  health-adjacent categories.) Build a banned/safe phrasing list BEFORE creative production.
- Which ad-standards bodies apply per geo (FTC, ASA, ASCI…) and what are the influencer
  disclosure rules?

## 2. Purchase cycle & unit economics
- Impulse (<1 day), considered (1-4 weeks), or long-cycle (1-12 months)?
- AOV/LTV and margin → sets CPA ceiling and channel viability. High LTV justifies
  higher CPA and longer windows; low AOV demands cheap reach + high CVR.
- One-time, repeat, or subscription? Subscription verticals optimize to trial→paid, not
  first purchase.

## 3. Creative codes that convert
- What does the winning ad in this category LOOK like? (Study 20+ competitor ads in Meta
  Ads Library / Google Transparency Center before concepting.)
- Which proof types matter: demos, testimonials, authority figures, certifications,
  before/after (where allowed), numbers?
- UGC-tolerance: does the category reward raw authenticity (beauty, food) or polished trust
  (finance, healthcare, luxury)?

## 4. Benchmark bands
- Pull category CPM/CPC/CVR/ROAS benchmarks for the target geo before setting client
  expectations. Judge performance against category medians, not global averages.

## 5. Measurement model
- Ecom: pixel + CAPI, catalog, purchase optimization.
- Lead gen: lead quality feedback loop (offline conversion import; optimize to qualified,
  not raw leads).
- App: event optimization (activation, not install).
- Offline/footfall: call tracking, store visits, promo codes, ticketing click-outs —
  set attribution expectations honestly (directional, not deterministic).

## 6. Seasonality & demand rhythm
- Category peaks (gifting seasons, enrollment windows, weather-driven, event-driven)?
- Is demand created (interruption) or harvested (search)? That sets the upper/lower funnel
  budget ratio.

# Part 2 — Industry quick guides

## Food & Beverage / Restaurants
Appetite appeal is the whole game: close-up food shots, sizzle/steam motion, <15s. Local
radius targeting + delivery-platform integration; UGC reviews and "order now" CTWA/WhatsApp
flows in emerging markets. HFSS restrictions (UK) and health-claim rules apply to packaged
food. Peaks: festivals, match days, weekends (dayparting matters).

## Health & Wellness (supplements, fitness, clinics)
Heavily policed: no disease/cure claims, no unrealistic body transformation, personal-health
targeting restrictions. Testimonials need disclaimers; clinics face medical-advertising
rules per country. Optimize lead gen to booked consultations (offline import), not form
fills.

## Finance & Insurance
Special/restricted category on most platforms (verification required in many geos; special
ad category flags limit targeting). Compliance sign-off on every asset; mandated
disclosures/APR language. Trust creative: authority, security signals, education-first
funnels. Long windows; optimize to funded/approved accounts via offline import.

## Real Estate
Housing = special category in the US (no age/gender/zip discrimination). Lead gen with
instant forms + WhatsApp works globally; virtual tours/walkthrough video outperform
stills. Geo-farming: radius + income proxies where allowed. Long cycle — nurture sequences
and remarketing pools matter more than first-click CPA.

## Education & Courses
Enrollment-window seasonality (plan backwards from intake dates). Lead gen → counselor
call → enrollment: optimize to qualified stage via offline import. Proof creative: outcomes,
placements, alumni stories. Special-category rules apply in some geos for employment-linked
claims.

## Travel & Hospitality
Long dreaming→booking cycle: Demand Gen/video for inspiration, search for capture,
retargeting with dynamic ads for dates/routes. Seasonal booking curves per source market;
price-drop and scarcity messaging works but must be truthful. UGC destination content
outperforms brand film for consideration.

## Automotive
Long cycle, high AOV: video model showcases + local dealer lead gen (test drives via
instant forms). Optimize to showroom visits/test-drive bookings via offline import.
Launch moments and festival exchange-offers (India) drive spikes.

## Gaming & Apps
App campaigns (Google UAC, Meta AAA) + TikTok playables where available. Optimize to
in-app events (activation, level-3, purchase), never installs. Creative velocity extreme:
gameplay-first UGC, hooks <1s, refresh weekly. Watch store-platform policy + age ratings.

## B2B / SaaS
Search intent capture + Demand Gen + founder-brand content. Optimize to SQL/opportunity via
offline import; judge on pipeline cohorts (30-90 days), not 7-day ROAS. Case-study and
comparison creative; niche targeting is a legitimate exception to broad-audience doctrine.

## Local Services (salons, repair, clinics, gyms)
Radius targeting + click-to-call + WhatsApp booking; Google Local Services Ads where
available. Reviews are the conversion engine — feed review velocity. Budget small, creative
simple, dayparting to open hours.

## Luxury & Jewelry
Brand-first: polished creative, no discount framing (protect price integrity). Wedding and
gifting seasonality dominates (region-specific wedding calendars). High-AOV consideration:
retargeting pools, lookbook video, store-visit measurement. Authenticity/certification
messaging (hallmarks, GIA) builds trust.

## Electronics & Gadgets
Spec-plus-story creative: lead with the life benefit, support with the spec. Launch-moment
spikes + festival/sale-event pricing (EMI/financing offers lift CVR in price-sensitive
markets). Comparison and unboxing UGC strong; watch warranty/claims wording.

# Part 3 — Worked examples (full-depth playbooks)

## Worked example: Skincare / Beauty

**Compliance first — this vertical gets ad accounts banned.**

Claims language (Meta + Google + ASCI/ASA aligned):
- BANNED framing: "treats/cures acne/eczema/psoriasis/rosacea", disease/medical claims,
  guaranteed transformation, "clinically proven" without substantiation on file.
- Meta hard rules: no before/after implying unlikely results; before/after prohibited for
  weight-loss and anti-aging/wrinkle products; no negative self-perception angles.
- SAFE framing: "helps soothe", "supports", "visibly reduces the appearance of",
  "dermatologically tested" (with test on file), sensory language.
- India ASCI: 500+ beauty brands flagged 2025-26 for exaggerated claims, misleading
  "natural"/"Ayurvedic" use, and missing influencer disclosure. Influencer statements =
  brand claims — the brand answers for them.
- UK ASA: filters must not exaggerate product effect; efficacy claims need substantiation.
- Cosmetic vs drug line: the moment a claim is therapeutic, it becomes a drug claim in most
  jurisdictions. Stay cosmetic.

**Strategy:**
- UGC-led: creator testimonials, texture/application ASMR, GRWM formats, derm-adjacent
  authority content. UGC ≈ 24% higher ROAS, ~47% lower CPM vs studio creative.
- Advantage+ Shopping primary + catalog for range sellers; hero-SKU campaigns for the
  flagship.
- Ingredient education works (actives, heritage ingredients) — pair ingredient + proof point.
- Routine-based bundling lifts AOV; subscription push in mature markets.

## Worked example: Clothing / Fashion

**2026 Meta benchmarks (fashion/apparel):** median ROAS 2.18x; good = 2.2-3.0x; top quartile
4.4x; top decile 6.0x. Judge against these, not vanity targets.

**Structure:**
- 70% budget: catalog-based Advantage+ Shopping on the full catalog (~4.52x vs 3.70x manual).
- 30% budget: hero-product / drop campaigns (new collections, seasonal stories).
- Catalog retargeting ~4.7x vs ~3.1x prospecting — feed health (images, titles,
  availability, price sync) is a weekly check.

**Creative engine:**
- 21+ new creatives/month minimum; at $5k+/day, 50-100 active creatives and 25-30% refresh
  every 2 weeks.
- Mix: on-model UGC try-ons, flat-lay + motion, street-style, size-inclusive representation,
  fit/fabric close-ups, styling videos ("3 ways to wear").
- Sizing + returns clarity in ad/LP reduces CPA and COD refusals.

**Seasonality:** BFCM (US/UK/EU), 11.11/12.12 (SEA), festival capsules + wedding season
(South Asia), Raya (MY/ID). Never start learning phase inside a peak.

## Worked example: Film / Entertainment

**Phase-gated campaign arc:**
1. **Announcement/title reveal** — poster day; fan amplification; search/knowledge-panel
   hygiene.
2. **Music-first** (where the industry works that way, e.g., Indian cinema) — single launch
   as its own campaign event; audio seeding on Reels/Shorts/TikTok so the sound trends.
3. **Teaser → Trailer** — trailer launch as live event; vertical cutdowns; meme-page seeding
   within hours (supply meme-able frames/dialogues deliberately).
4. **Release week** — opening-day energy content, booking-intent search campaigns
   ("<title> tickets" + ticketing deep links), creator review seeding, city-level geo pushes.
5. **Sustain/streaming window** — dialogue clips, BTS; streaming-release re-launch campaign
   with platform co-marketing.

**Mix (adjust per market):** YouTube 40-50% (trailer + Shorts), Meta 35-45% (Reels, memes,
creator whitelisting), Search/Display 10-15% (title + booking intent). TikTok where
available (NOT India).

**Craft:** every asset vertical-first; hook = star/dialogue/music in <2s; language-split
everything (each dub is a separate campaign with separate audiences); box-office attribution
is directional — set that expectation with producers upfront.

## Worked example: Tech (SaaS / consumer tech / apps)

**B2B SaaS:** Search + AI Max 40-50% (intent capture), Demand Gen + YouTube 20-30%, Meta
20-30% (retargeting, founder-led content, case studies). Offline conversion import (CRM
stage changes) is the single biggest lever — optimize toward SQL/opportunity, not raw leads.
Consent language per geo (GDPR/DPDP/PDPA). Judge on 30-90 day pipeline cohorts.

**Consumer tech / apps:** App campaigns with event optimization on activation, not install.
Feature-demo UGC ("watch me do X in 10 seconds") outperforms spec-sheet creative.
Price-sensitive markets: financing/EMI framing materially lifts CVR.

# Cross-vertical creative production stack (adapt to your tooling)

- Concepting: ad-copy/copywriting skills; angle matrix per client (fear/desire/proof/
  identity/urgency × formats).
- Video: AI video/UGC generation tooling; static/brand: Canva or equivalent with brand kits.
- Landing pages: your frontend/UI skills of choice.
- Compliance pass: Part 1 framework + platform policy references before every batch upload.
